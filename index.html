The Fellowship

Built for high school researchers

Atlas is a research fellowship, not a club. Fellows train with university researchers, collect real data, publish through partner journals, and connect with policy organizations doing real work on education equity.

01

Published Research

Co-author credit on the cross-national dataset. Write a policy brief and submit it to our partner journals for publication. Your name on real academic work.

02

Learn from Researchers

Professors at Stanford and other leading universities work with our fellows directly. You hear how research actually works, ask questions, and build connections you wouldn't get on your own.

03

Policy Connections

We work with policy organizations in education. Fellows get exposure to how advocacy works and can connect their research to people doing real work on education equity.

04

A Global Network

Fellows work alongside student researchers around the world. You're paired with someone in a different country to compare what you found. The differences in the data tell the real story.

The Process

A six-week research fellowship

Every fellow follows the same structured program. No prior research experience required. Atlas trains you in the methodology.

01

Selected as a school research lead

Apply to represent your school. Selected fellows get orientation materials and train in research methodology with professors at Stanford and other leading universities.

02

Train in the methodology

Learn how to run a standardized research instrument. Practice the methodology. Build a team of at least five members at your school for data collection.

03

Collect data at your school

Deploy the Atlas survey across your school community. Your data joins a global dataset alongside schools in countries across the Global South and Global North.

04

Write, publish, and connect

Write a policy brief comparing your school's findings to the global dataset. Submit it to our partner journals for publication. Connect with the policy organizations we work with so your research contributes to real education advocacy.

The Study

AI, Education, and Knowledge Access

A cross-national survey examining how AI is reorganizing who gets to access, produce, and share knowledge. The same instrument deployed in Dhaka, Danville, and Jakarta reveals how cultural context shapes the impact of technology on learning.

What We Measure

A student in rural Nepal using AI to access information their school cannot provide is a fundamentally different story than a student in California whose teacher built it into the curriculum. Atlas measures that difference: how students access AI tools, who introduces them to new technologies, whether their families and schools support AI-assisted learning, and how trust in AI-generated knowledge shifts across cultures.

Why It Matters

AI is framed as a universal equalizer. Our data shows structural access, cultural attitudes, and institutional support shape whether AI opens doors or widens gaps.

The Output

A cross-national dataset, a synthesis analysis authored by Atlas leadership, and student-authored policy briefs submitted through our partner journals for publication. Your work has a path forward from day one.

Built for Rigor

Atlas uses a standardized instrument across every school. The methodology is designed for real academic credibility. Primary data collection at a global scale, led by high school researchers.
