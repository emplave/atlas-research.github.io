The Study

AI, Education, and Knowledge Access

A cross-national survey examining how AI is reorganizing who gets to access, produce, and share knowledge. The same instrument deployed in Dhaka, Danville, and Jakarta reveals how cultural context shapes the impact of technology on learning.

What We Measure

How students access AI tools, who introduces them to new technologies, whether their schools and families support AI-assisted learning, and how trust in AI-generated knowledge differs across cultures. The instrument captures both structural access and cultural attitudes toward technology and knowledge production.

Why It Matters

AI is framed as a universal equalizer. Our data shows structural access, cultural attitudes, and institutional support shape whether AI opens doors or widens gaps.

The Output

A cross-national dataset, a synthesis analysis authored by Atlas leadership, and student-authored policy briefs published in the Atlas Journal of Education Policy.

Built for Rigor

Atlas uses a standardized instrument across every school. The methodology is designed for real academic credibility. Primary data collection at a global scale, led by high school researchers.

Cross-National Design

A student in rural Nepal using AI to access information their school cannot provide is a fundamentally different story than a student in California whose teacher built AI into the curriculum. That difference is what we measure.

The Fellowship

Built for high school researchers

Atlas is a research fellowship, not a club. Fellows are selected to lead primary research at their school and join a global network of student researchers.

01

Published Research

Co-author credit on the cross-national dataset. Fellows who write a policy brief are published in the Atlas Journal of Education Policy.

02

Access to Researchers

Monthly webinars with university professors and policy professionals. Direct exposure to people and institutions most high school students don't have access to.

03

A Global Network

Fellows work alongside student researchers around the world. An international study producing real academic and policy output.

04

Real Policy Impact

Research from Atlas fellows feeds into advocacy organizations and education policy conversations, turning local findings into global impact.

The Process

A six-week research fellowship

Every fellow follows the same structured program. No prior research experience required. Atlas trains you in the methodology.

01

Selected as a school research lead

Apply to represent your school. Selected fellows receive orientation materials and a recorded training webinar from a university researcher.

02

Train in research methodology

Learn how to administer a standardized research instrument, practice the methodology, and build a team of at least five members at your school for data collection.

03

Lead data collection at your school

Deploy the Atlas survey across your school community. The same instrument used in Bangladesh, Canada, Saudi Arabia, and Indonesia. Your data joins a global dataset.

04

Publish your findings

Submit your data to Atlas. Take it further by authoring a policy brief comparing your findings to the global dataset and submitting it for publication in the Atlas Journal.
