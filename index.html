The Study

AI, Education, and Knowledge Access

A cross-national survey examining how artificial intelligence is reorganizing who gets to access, produce, and share knowledge. The same instrument deployed in Dhaka, Danville, and Jakarta reveals how cultural context shapes the impact of technology on learning.

What We're Measuring

How students access AI tools, who introduces them to new technologies, whether their schools and families support AI-assisted learning, and how trust in AI-generated knowledge differs across cultures.

Why It Matters

AI is often framed as a universal equalizer. Our data shows the reality is more complex. Structural access, cultural attitudes, and institutional support shape whether AI opens doors or widens gaps.

The Output

A cross-national dataset, a synthesis analysis authored by Atlas leadership, and student-authored policy briefs published in the Atlas Journal of Education Policy.

Academic Credibility

Findings from Atlas research have been submitted for presentation at the American Anthropological Association 2026 Annual Meeting. The study is grounded in real methodology, not performative research.

For Student Researchers

What you get

Atlas is a research position, not a club. Representatives are selected to lead data collection at their school and join a global network of student researchers.

01

Published Research

Co-contributor credit on the cross-national dataset. Students who write a policy brief are published in the Atlas Journal of Education Policy. "Published researcher in an international education policy journal" on your application.

02

Access to Researchers

Monthly webinars with university professors and policy professionals. Direct exposure to people and institutions most students can't reach on their own.

03

A Global Network

Work alongside student researchers in 15+ countries. Atlas representatives aren't doing a school project. They're contributing to an international study with real academic output.

The Process

Six weeks. One study. Real output.

Every representative follows the same structured program. No prior research experience required. We teach you the methodology.

01

Apply and onboard

Submit your application to represent your school. Selected representatives receive orientation materials and a recorded training webinar from a university researcher.

02

Learn the instrument

Understand the survey methodology, practice administering it, and recruit at least five club members at your school to participate in data collection.

03

Deploy the survey

Distribute the standardized Atlas survey at your school. The same questions asked in Bangladesh, Canada, Saudi Arabia, and Indonesia. Your school's data joins a global dataset.

04

Submit and publish

Submit your data to Atlas. Students who want to go further can write a policy brief comparing their school's findings to the global dataset and submit it for publication in the Atlas Journal.
