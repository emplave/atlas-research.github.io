First issue: Fall 2026. The Atlas Journal is preparing to accept submissions from Atlas Fellows. Full submission guidelines will be published when the fellowship opens.

About the Journal

The Atlas Journal of Education Policy is Atlas's in-house publication for student-authored policy briefs and literature reviews on education access. Every piece comes from work produced in the Atlas Fellowship, built from existing data — UNESCO, the World Bank, PISA, national education records — and analyzed by the fellow.

The journal exists because student research should have somewhere to go. It sits alongside the external journals fellows also submit to, including the International Journal of High School Research and Curieux Review, and gives Atlas a venue of its own.

Scope

The journal publishes briefs and short analyses on education access, including:

  • School funding and resource gaps
  • Teacher shortages and their effects on learning
  • Language of instruction and mother-tongue schooling
  • Refugee and post-conflict education
  • Gender gaps in schooling
  • Rural and urban divides in access
  • Access to technology, including AI, as one factor among many

Submission

Submissions are open to Atlas Fellows who complete the fellowship. Briefs and literature reviews are drawn from a fellow's own research on an education-access question. Full length and format requirements will be set out in the submission guidelines before the first issue.

Review

Each submission is read by the Atlas editorial team, with outside reviewers brought in where relevant expertise is available. Review is formative: work that needs revision comes back with written feedback and a chance to resubmit. Not everything submitted is published.
