About the Journal

The Atlas Journal of Education Policy publishes student-authored policy briefs analyzing how AI is reshaping education access across cultural contexts. Every piece is based on primary data collected through the Atlas Research Fellowship, where students in multiple countries deploy a standardized survey instrument at their schools.

The journal exists because student research should go somewhere. Policy briefs published here represent the analysis of real data, collected by real students, in real schools around the world.

Scope

The journal publishes policy briefs and short analyses at the intersection of:

  • AI adoption and education access across cultural contexts
  • Structural barriers to research literacy and knowledge production
  • The role of families, schools, and institutions in mediating technology access
  • Trust in AI-generated knowledge across the Global South and Global North
  • Policy responses to the intersection of AI and education equity

Submission

Submissions are open to Atlas Research Fellows who have completed the fellowship program and collected data using the Atlas survey instrument. All submissions are reviewed by Atlas leadership and, where possible, by external reviewers with relevant expertise.

Policy briefs should be 500–2,000 words and should analyze the fellow's school-level data in comparison to the global dataset. Fellows are encouraged to connect their findings to local education policy contexts.

Review Process

Each submission is reviewed for clarity of argument, quality of data analysis, and relevance to education policy. The review process is designed to be formative. Fellows whose submissions require revisions will receive written feedback and the opportunity to resubmit.
