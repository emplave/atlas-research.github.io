Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

Atlas Research Institute is a youth-led research organization. We take the privacy of our applicants, fellows, and participants seriously. This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding that data.

What we collect

When you apply to the Atlas Research Fellowship or participate in Atlas programs, we may collect:

  • Name, email address, school name, city, and country
  • Grade level and self-reported GPA
  • Extracurricular activities and research experience
  • Responses to application questions
  • Survey data collected as part of the Atlas study (aggregated, not individually identifiable)

How we use your data

We use the data we collect for the following purposes:

  • Reviewing and processing fellowship applications
  • Communicating with fellows about program activities, deadlines, and updates
  • Aggregating survey data for research analysis (individual responses are never published)
  • Publishing aggregate findings in the Atlas Journal of Education Policy and related research outputs
  • Improving the Atlas program and research methodology

What we do not do

  • We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for marketing or commercial purposes
  • We do not publish individually identifiable data in any research output
  • We do not use your data for any purpose other than those listed above

Who can access your data

Your personal data is accessible only to Atlas Research Institute leadership and designated program coordinators. Survey data is stored in aggregate form and accessed only for research purposes. We use Google Workspace (Google Sheets, Google Forms) to store and process data.

Data retention

We retain application data for the duration of your participation in Atlas programs and for up to 12 months after the program concludes, unless you request earlier deletion. Aggregated research data (which does not contain personally identifiable information) may be retained indefinitely for academic purposes.

Applicants under 18

Atlas primarily serves high school students. If you are under 18, we encourage you to inform a parent or guardian about your participation. Atlas does not knowingly collect data from children under 13. If we learn we have collected data from a child under 13, we will delete it promptly.

Your rights

You have the right to:

  • Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your personal data
  • Withdraw from the program at any time

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the email address below.

Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. Continued participation in Atlas programs after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact

For questions about this privacy policy or your data, contact us at atlas.research.org@gmail.com.
