Summer 2026 Fellowship

Apply to the Atlas Fellowship

A four-week mentored research program on global education access. You will work with a mentor to research a specific question about who gets access to education and why, and turn it into a literature review written to the standard of a peer-reviewed journal, with a path to submission for publication.

No prior research experience is required. We care about how you think, not what you have already done.

Duration 4 weeks Format Remote Cost Free Deadline Jul 24

Applications are reviewed as they arrive, and decisions go out on a rolling basis. The cohort begins August 3, 2026. Applying earlier is better than applying late.

How you think and workthis is what we read most closely
Where you live, who gets a good education and who gets left behind? Describe one specific problem like this that you have noticed.
This could be about who can afford school, who lives too far from a good one, who has to work instead, who speaks the wrong language in class, or anything else you have seen. Write in your own voice. There is no right answer and no expected vocabulary. We want to see how you notice a problem and how you think about it.

How would you try to find out if this is really happening, and how big it is?
You might look for numbers someone has already collected, like government or school data or a UN report. Or you might describe how you would gather your own, like a survey or interviews. There is no single right source. We want to see how you would start looking.
Tell us about something you started on your own and finished.
We want to know that you finish what you start. Tell us about something you set out to do and saw through to the end — a project, a goal, something you taught yourself, something you built or fixed or organized. What made it hard, and how did you get it done?
